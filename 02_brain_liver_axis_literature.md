# Brain-Liver Axis in Traumatic Brain Injury: Literature Review

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Focus:** Lipid droplet-accumulating microglia, DHA-containing lipids, brain-liver crosstalk, IL-6 signaling, peripheral lipid origin

---

## 1. Lipid Droplet-Accumulating Microglia (LDAM) after TBI

### 1.1 Seminal Discovery of LDAM

The concept of lipid-droplet-accumulating microglia (LDAM) was established by **Marschallinger et al. (2020)** in a landmark study. LDAM are characterized by:
- **Defective phagocytosis** (impaired debris clearance)
- **High ROS production** (reactive oxygen species)
- **Pro-inflammatory cytokine secretion**
- A **transcriptional profile driven by innate inflammation**, distinct from previously known microglial states (e.g., DAM/disease-associated microglia)

**Key genes/pathways identified via CRISPR-Cas9 screen:**
- **GRN (Progranulin)** -- variants cause autosomal-dominant neurodegeneration; loss promotes LD accumulation
- Lipid metabolism genes, innate immune signaling genes
- The LDAM transcriptome is enriched for lipid storage, oxidative stress, and inflammatory signaling pathways

> Marschallinger J, Iram T, Zardeneta M, et al. *Lipid-droplet-accumulating microglia represent a dysfunctional and proinflammatory state in the aging brain.* **Nat Neurosci.** 2020;23(2):194-208. **PMID: 31959936** | DOI: 10.1038/s41593-019-0566-1

### 1.2 TDP-43 Condensates and Lipid Droplets in Microglia After TBI

**Zambusi et al. (2022)** used a zebrafish stab wound injury model and human postmortem TBI cortical tissues:
- TBI induces an injury-specific microglial state with **co-accumulation of lipid droplets and TDP-43+ condensates**
- **Granulin (GRN)**-mediated clearance of both LDs and TDP-43 condensates is necessary for microglia to return to a basal state
- Without GRN-mediated clearance, microglia remain chronically activated, preventing scarless regeneration
- In **human postmortem TBI cortex**, extent of microglial activation correlated with LD and TDP-43+ condensate accumulation

> Zambusi A, Novoselc KT, Hutten S, et al. *TDP-43 condensates and lipid droplets regulate the reactivity of microglia and regeneration after traumatic brain injury.* **Nat Neurosci.** 2022;25(12). **PMID: 36424432** | DOI: 10.1038/s41593-022-01199-y

### 1.3 FASN Acetylation Drives Microglial LD Accumulation After TBI

**Zhang et al. (2026)** identified the molecular mechanism of LD formation in microglia post-TBI:
- **Hyperacetylation of FASN (fatty acid synthase) at lysine K673** is a critical driver of LD formation
- This acetylation enhances both inflammatory cytokine secretion and phagocytic activity
- **HDAC3** is identified as the deacetylating enzyme for this site
- Mice with **FASN K673R mutation** (blocking acetylation) show reduced microglial lipid accumulation and neuroinflammation after TBI
- Suggests deacetylation pathways as a novel therapeutic target

> Zhang F, Lv T, Xu T, et al. *Acetylation of fatty acid synthase regulates microglial lipid droplets accumulation and pro-inflammatory activity following traumatic brain injury.* **Redox Biol.** 2026;79:103978. **PMID: 41412037** | DOI: 10.1016/j.redox.2025.103978

### 1.4 Autophagy-Lysosomal Dysfunction and Microglial Lipid Reprogramming

**Mehrabani-Tabari et al. (2025)** demonstrated via single-cell approaches:
- TBI triggers **widespread lipid metabolism reprogramming** across all microglial and monocyte populations
- Specific populations accumulate **neutral storage lipids (cholesteryl esters and triglycerides)** in both lipid droplets and lysosomes
- Accumulation results from **phagocytosis of myelin debris** rather than de novo lipid synthesis
- Impaired autophagy creates a **pathological feedback loop**: lipid phagocytosis inhibits autophagy-lysosomal function, which further exacerbates lipid retention
- Pharmacological inhibition of autophagy worsens the phenotype

> Mehrabani-Tabari AA, Hegdekar N, Herb BR, et al. *Inhibition of autophagy-lysosomal function exacerbates microglial and monocyte lipid metabolism reprograming and dysfunction after brain injury.* **Research Square [Preprint].** 2025. **PMID: 41282218** | DOI: 10.21203/rs.3.rs-7682363/v1

### 1.5 Additional LDAM References

- **Functional hydrogel for modulating lipid droplets and neuroinflammation in head injury** -- Chen F et al. Adv Funct Mater. 2025. **PMID: 40857429** (therapeutic hydrogel sequestering cholesterol to reduce microglial LD)
- **RetSat Knockout Mitigates Hypoxia-Induced Microglial Activation by Enhancing Lipid Droplets Degradation** -- Hu W et al. Glia. 2026. **PMID: 41388745**
- **Microglial lipid droplets as therapeutic targets in age-related neurodegenerative diseases** -- Sung S et al. NPJ Aging. 2025. **PMID: 41318530** (review)
- **Assessment and Quantification of Foam Cells and Lipid Droplet-Accumulating Microglia in Mouse Brain Tissue Using BODIPY Staining** -- Maiyo BK et al. Bio Protoc. 2024. **PMID: 39525975** (methods paper)

### 1.6 Timeline of LD Formation and Clearance

Based on available data, the timeline is approximately:
- **Acute phase (hours to days)**: Myelin debris phagocytosis begins; lipid metabolism gene expression changes detectable within 24h
- **Subacute phase (3-7 days)**: Significant LD accumulation in microglia; TDP-43 condensates form
- **Chronic phase (weeks to months)**: Without GRN-mediated clearance, LDs persist; in aging, clearance is further impaired
- **Resolution** (in models with intact clearance): GRN-dependent, requires functional autophagy-lysosomal pathway
- Key insight: In zebrafish (Zambusi 2022), clearance is rapid and enables scarless regeneration; in mammals, clearance is slower/incomplete, contributing to chronic neuroinflammation

## 2. DHA-Containing Phospholipids and Triglycerides in Brain Injury

### 2.1 DHA-Containing Phospholipids Decline After TBI

**Fernandez et al. (2025)** -- Aging effects on lipid metabolism in TBI:
- **Phospholipids with 6 double bonds (predicted DHA-containing) steadily declined during recovery** from TBI
- Total fatty acid analysis confirmed **decreased DHA at 28 days post-injury (dpi)** in aged mice, more severely after TBI
- Acylcarnitines increased ~3-fold acutely
- Aged brains showed greater lipid content changes despite less transcriptomic impact

> Fernandez RF, Weglarczyk P, Zokaei S, et al. *Aging Effects on Lipid Metabolism in Response to Traumatic Brain Injury.* **J Neurochem.** 2025;169(9):e70227. **PMID: 40939097** | DOI: 10.1111/jnc.70227

**Abdullah et al. (2014)** -- Injury-specific phospholipid changes 3 months post-TBI:
- Decreased cortical and cerebellar **phosphatidylcholine (PC) and phosphatidylethanolamine (PE)** levels
- Hippocampal PC, sphingomyelin, and PE were elevated
- **PUFA-containing lipid species, particularly DHA-containing phospholipids, were reduced** in injured brains
- Suggests chronic phospholipid remodeling post-TBI

> Abdullah L, Evans JE, Ferguson S, et al. *Lipidomic analyses identify injury-specific phospholipid changes 3 mo after traumatic brain injury.* **FASEB J.** 2014;28(12):5311-21. **PMID: 25208845** | DOI: 10.1096/fj.14-258228

**Emmerich et al. (2016)** -- Plasma lipidomics in military mTBI:
- Plasma lipidomic profiling in military mild TBI with APOE e4-dependent effects
- Altered phospholipid profiles in TBI patients

> Emmerich T, Abdullah L, Crynen G, et al. *Plasma Lipidomic Profiling in a Military Population of Mild Traumatic Brain Injury...* **J Neurotrauma.** 2016. **PMID: 26714394**

### 2.2 DHA/n-3 PUFA Supplementation in TBI

**Mondal et al. (2021)** -- Systemic n-3 PUFA elevation protects against TBI:
- Fat1+ transgenic mice (endogenously elevated n-3 PUFA) were **resistant to motor, emotional, and visual deficits** after blast TBI
- Protection mediated via **suppressed ceramide biosynthesis** and reduced neuroinflammation

> Mondal K, Takahashi H, Cole J 2nd, et al. *Systemic Elevation of n-3 Polyunsaturated Fatty Acids (n-3-PUFA) Is Associated with Protection against Visual, Motor, and Emotional Deficits in Mice following Closed-Head Mild Traumatic Brain Injury.* **Mol Neurobiol.** 2021;58(11). **PMID: 34365584** | DOI: 10.1007/s12035-021-02501-y

**Mondal et al. (2025)** -- Oral n-3 PUFA supplementation prevents TBI deficits:
> Mondal K, Gary AA, Dash A, et al. *Oral Supplementation of n-3 Polyunsaturated Fatty Acids (n-3-PUFA) Can Prevent TBI-Induced Visual, Motor, and Emotional Deficits in Mice.* **Mol Neurobiol.** 2025. **PMID: 40346443**

**Niryana et al. (2025)** -- Clinical trial of omega-3 in head injury:
> Niryana IW, et al. *Impact of omega-3 supplementation on inflammatory markers and clinical outcomes in patients with moderate-to-severe head injuries.* **Surg Neurol Int.** 2025. **PMID: 41036044**

### 2.3 Key Insight on DHA-Triglyceride Connection

While direct evidence for **DHA-containing triglyceride upregulation** in brain after TBI is limited, Mehrabani-Tabari et al. (2025) showed that microglial lipid droplets after TBI contain **neutral storage lipids including triglycerides and cholesteryl esters**. The composition likely reflects phagocytosed myelin-derived lipids, which are rich in DHA-containing phospholipids. The brain may convert phagocytosed DHA-phospholipids into DHA-TG for storage in lipid droplets.

## 3. Brain-Liver Crosstalk After TBI

### 3.1 TBI Disrupts Hepatic Lipid Metabolism

**Rege et al. (2019)** -- Key paper directly addressing brain-liver lipid axis in TBI:
- TBI affects **glucose metabolism and signaling proteins for insulin and growth hormone in the liver**
- Fructose amplifies TBI-induced hepatic metabolic disruption
- TBI promotes **liver lipid accumulation**
- The **hypothalamic-pituitary-growth axis** regulates these peripheral changes
- Inflammatory markers, oxidative stress, and metabolic dysfunction increase in liver after TBI

> Rege SD, Royes L, Tsai B, et al. *Brain Trauma Disrupts Hepatic Lipid Metabolism: Blame It on Fructose?* **Mol Nutr Food Res.** 2019;63(15):e1801054. **PMID: 31087499** | DOI: 10.1002/mnfr.201801054

### 3.2 Brain-Gut-Liver Axis After TBI

**Amaral et al. (2024)** -- Probiotic therapy modulates brain-gut-liver axis post-TBI:
- TBI causes gut microbiota dysbiosis that affects liver function
- Demonstrates bidirectional communication: brain injury affects gut and liver, gut/liver changes feed back to brain

> Amaral WZ, Kokroko N, Treangen TJ, et al. *Probiotic therapy modulates the brain-gut-liver microbiota axis in a mouse model of traumatic brain injury.* **Biochim Biophys Acta Mol Basis Dis.** 2024. **PMID: 39209236**

### 3.3 Systemic Organ Crosstalk Review

**Battaglini et al. (2024)** -- Comprehensive review:
- Acute brain injury triggers **neuroinflammation and neurodegeneration** causing multi-organ inflammation
- Liver dysfunction is recognized as a consequence of brain injury
- Mechanisms include sympathetic nervous system activation, HPA axis, and circulating inflammatory mediators

> Battaglini D, De Rosa S, Godoy DA. *Crosstalk Between the Nervous System and Systemic Organs in Acute Brain Injury.* **Neurocrit Care.** 2024;40(1):337-348. **PMID: 37081275** | DOI: 10.1007/s12028-023-01725-1

### 3.4 TBI-Induced Multi-Organ Dysfunction

**Thakur et al. (2025)** -- Multi-organ dysfunction after TBI:
> Thakur M, Sharma S, et al. *Eprosartan alleviates the traumatic brain injury-induced multi-organ dysfunction syndrome in mice.* **Mol Cell Neurosci.** 2025. **PMID: 40886946** | DOI: 10.1016/j.mcn.2025.104035

### 3.5 Does the Liver Supply DHA to the Injured Brain?

**Direct evidence is currently limited but circumstantial evidence is strong:**

1. The liver is the **primary site of DHA synthesis** from alpha-linolenic acid (ALA) via elongases and desaturases
2. Hepatic DHA is exported as **LPC-DHA (lysophosphatidylcholine-DHA)** into plasma
3. Brain uptake of DHA depends on **MFSD2A transporter** at the blood-brain barrier
4. After brain injury, DHA-containing phospholipids are depleted (Fernandez 2025; Abdullah 2014)
5. Systemic n-3 PUFA elevation (Fat1 mice or supplementation) protects the brain after TBI (Mondal 2021, 2025)
6. **This strongly suggests the liver must replenish brain DHA pools after injury, but this specific pathway has not been directly demonstrated in TBI models**

## 4. IL-6 Signaling in Brain-Liver Axis

### 4.1 IL-6 Mediates Hepatic Acute Phase Response After TBI

**Yang et al. (2013)** -- Direct evidence of IL-6/liver axis post-TBI:
- Haptoglobin protein and mRNA levels **increased after TBI in both serum and liver, especially liver**
- **IL-6 is the key mediator** of hepatic haptoglobin induction after TBI
- Liver is the primary source of elevated serum acute phase proteins after brain injury

> Yang S, Ma Y, Liu Y, et al. *Elevated serum haptoglobin after traumatic brain injury is synthesized mainly in liver.* **J Neurosci Res.** 2013;91(2):230-9. **PMID: 23172820** | DOI: 10.1002/jnr.23159

### 4.2 IL-6/GP130 Signaling Protects Against Hepatic Lipid Droplet Accumulation

**Carbonaro et al. (2023)** -- Critical finding on IL-6-GP130-hepatic lipid axis:
- In humanized liver models, **incompatible IL-6 signaling leads to hepatic lipid droplet accumulation (steatosis)**
- Restoration of **IL-6-GP130 signaling substantially reduced hepatosteatosis**
- This demonstrates that IL-6 signaling is **anti-steatotic** in hepatocytes -- it promotes lipid catabolism/export
- Implies: after TBI, brain-derived IL-6 reaching the liver via circulation could mobilize hepatic lipid metabolism

> Carbonaro M, Wang K, Huang H, et al. *IL-6-GP130 signaling protects human hepatocytes against lipid droplet accumulation in humanized liver models.* **Sci Adv.** 2023;9(15):eadf4490. **PMID: 37058568** | DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.adf4490

### 4.3 IL-6/JAK/STAT3 in Hepatic Lipid Metabolism

- **Dantas et al. (2025)**: JAK-STAT3 signaling upregulated in liver driving acute phase response and impairing lipid metabolism. **PMID: 41278737**
- **Wang et al. (2026)**: Palmitic acid-induced lipotoxicity involves **p38/MAPK and JAK/STAT pathways** with raised IL-6 in hepatocytes. **PMID: 41285220**
- **Arneson-Wissink et al. (2024)**: Hepatic STAT3 signaling drives early-stage cachexia with altered lipid metabolism. **PMID: 38632714**

### 4.4 Hepatic IL-6RA Knockout and Brain Recovery

**No direct evidence found** for hepatic IL-6RA conditional knockout affecting brain recovery after TBI. This represents a **significant knowledge gap** and potential novel research direction. However, the following logic chain supports this hypothesis:
1. TBI elevates circulating IL-6
2. IL-6 activates hepatic STAT3 via IL-6R/GP130
3. Hepatic IL-6 signaling regulates lipid metabolism (Carbonaro 2023)
4. Liver is the primary DHA source for the brain
5. Therefore, hepatic IL-6RA knockout could impair liver lipid mobilization and DHA supply to the injured brain

## 5. Peripheral Origin of Brain Lipids After Injury

### 5.1 MFSD2A: The Gateway for Peripheral DHA Entry to Brain

The major facilitator superfamily domain-containing protein 2A (MFSD2A) is the primary transporter for circulating DHA into the brain:

- **Blades et al. (2025)**: Comprehensive review of MFSD2A molecular mechanism for omega-3 transport. **PMID: 40126401**
- **Wong & Silver (2020)**: MFSD2A as a physiologically important lysolipid transporter. **PMID: 32705603**
- DHA enters the brain as **LPC-DHA** (lysophosphatidylcholine-DHA), transported by MFSD2A at the BBB endothelium
- **Iwao et al. (2023)**: Aging decreases DHA transport across BBB in mice. **PMID: 36795730**

### 5.2 MFSD2A After Brain Injury

- **Zhao et al. (2020)**: MFSD2A expression **decreases significantly after subarachnoid hemorrhage**, reaching lowest at 72h. This could impair DHA entry when it is most needed. **PMID: 31907728**
- **Li et al. (2023)**: Mfsd2a attenuates hypoxic-ischemic brain damage via BBB protection. **PMID: 36820986**

### 5.3 Evidence for Peripheral Origin of Microglial Lipid Droplets

**Direct evidence is limited, but key findings suggest a mixed origin:**

1. **Myelin-derived lipids (endogenous)**: Mehrabani-Tabari et al. (2025) showed that microglial LD accumulation results primarily from **phagocytosis of myelin debris** -- an endogenous brain source
2. **Peripheral contribution**: Given that:
   - BBB is disrupted after TBI, allowing peripheral lipid/lipoprotein entry
   - Plasma lipoproteins (VLDL, LDL) carry fatty acids including DHA
   - PCSK9 and high-fat diet synergistically induce neurovascular dysfunction (Zhang et al. 2025, PMID: 41278621)
   - Peripherally derived lipids could contribute to microglial LD via disrupted BBB
3. **Gap**: No study has used isotope-labeled peripheral fatty acids to directly trace their incorporation into microglial lipid droplets after TBI

## 7. Summary and Knowledge Gaps

### What Is Known:
1. **LDAM are a distinct dysfunctional microglial state** driven by lipid droplet accumulation, with defective phagocytosis and pro-inflammatory profile (Marschallinger 2020)
2. **TBI induces microglial LD accumulation** primarily via myelin debris phagocytosis and impaired autophagy (Mehrabani-Tabari 2025; Zambusi 2022)
3. **FASN acetylation (K673)** is a key molecular driver of LD formation post-TBI, regulated by HDAC3 (Zhang 2026)
4. **GRN/progranulin** is essential for LD clearance and microglial return to homeostasis (Zambusi 2022)
5. **DHA-containing phospholipids decline** in brain after TBI, especially with aging (Fernandez 2025; Abdullah 2014)
6. **TBI disrupts hepatic lipid metabolism** (Rege 2019), and liver responds with acute phase proteins via IL-6 (Yang 2013)
7. **IL-6/GP130 signaling is anti-steatotic** in hepatocytes (Carbonaro 2023)
8. **Peripheral DHA enters brain via MFSD2A/LPC-DHA pathway** (multiple refs), and this transport may be impaired after injury (Zhao 2020)
9. **Systemic n-3 PUFA elevation protects** against TBI deficits (Mondal 2021, 2025)

### Major Knowledge Gaps:
1. **No direct demonstration** that the liver supplies DHA to the injured brain in TBI models
2. **No liver transcriptomics/RNA-seq** datasets after TBI (massive gap)
3. **No hepatic IL-6RA conditional knockout** studies examining brain recovery
4. **No isotope tracing** of peripheral fatty acids into microglial lipid droplets
5. **Unknown whether brain LDs contain peripherally-derived vs. myelin-derived lipids** (likely both, proportion unknown)
6. **MFSD2A regulation after TBI** is poorly characterized (only studied in SAH and ischemia)
7. **The temporal dynamics** of brain-liver lipid communication after TBI are uncharacterized
8. **Whether hepatic lipid mobilization** (DHA-TG -> LPC-DHA export) is enhanced or impaired after TBI is unknown
